El Niño

El Niño is a climate pattern that describes the unusual warming of surface waters in the eastern tropical Pacific Ocean.

What Causes El Niño?

El Niño occurs when trade winds weaken. Warm water is pushed back east toward the west coast of the Americas.

This warm water changes global weather patterns and affects rainfall, storms, and temperatures.

Effects of El Niño

El Niño can cause heavy rainfall in some regions and droughts in others.

  • Flooding in South America
  • Drought in Australia
  • Warmer winters in North America
  • Reduced fish populations

Impact on Agriculture

Farmers may face crop failures because of changing rainfall patterns.

Marine Life

Warm ocean water reduces nutrients for marine organisms, impacting fisheries.

Climate Change Connection

Scientists are studying whether climate change increases the intensity of El Niño events.

Historical Events

The 1997–1998 El Niño caused major flooding and weather disruptions globally.

Conclusion

Understanding El Niño helps governments prepare for extreme weather events.
